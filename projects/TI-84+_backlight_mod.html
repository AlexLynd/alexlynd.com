TI-84+ Backlight Mod

Home Projects Resources Guides

Have you ever wanted to do your math homework play Super Mario on your calculator in the dark? Now you're in luck. I had initially intended to create this project using an EL pannel and a small inverter like Tyler Anderson did here, but found an old iPhone sitting around that I could take apart. Just before buying the parts, I took apart my friend's iPhone, and found a small diffusive plastic film and some micro LEDs that acted as the phone backlighting, and then I realized that I could repurpose my old, broken iPhone to give my calculator some light. It was given to me by a friend, and I had intents on fixing on it, but the calculator was a priority.

Materials:

  • TI-84+
  • iPhone Backlight

Instructions:

  1. Start by taking apart the calculator: I have a guide here, you will need a Torx T6 bit and some other parts.
  2. Carefully remove the motherboard using the documented process, removing the excess plastic and so forth.
  3. Ignore these following steps if you just bought the diffusive backlight.
